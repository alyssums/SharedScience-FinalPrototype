Physics

Physics is a natural science that involves the study of matter and energy and how they interact with one another. It is at its basic form the study of nature and how the universe behaves. Physics is considered one of the most fundamental studies of science because it deals with all types of science at its core.

Major Laws of Physics:


Newton’s Law of Motion: basic rules describing how the motion of objects change
Law of Gravity: Explanation of the attractive forces between two masses.
Conservation of Mass-Energy: Total energy in a closed system will remain constant.

Law of Thermodynamics:

  • Zeroeth law of thermodynamics makes the notion of temperature possible.
  • First law of thermodynamics demonstrates the relationship between internal energy, added heat, and work within a system.
  • Second law of thermodynamics relates to the natural flow of heat within a closed system.
  • Third law of thermodynamics states that it is impossible to create a thermodynamic process which is perfectly efficient.

Electrostatic Laws: Coulomb's law and Gauss's law of the relationship electrically charged particles

Invariance of the Speed of Light: The speed of light in a vacuum is constant
