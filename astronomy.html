Astronomy

Astronomy is a study of science that deals with the universe beyond Earth. Since astronomy is such a broad subject, it has been broken up into numerous subfields that focus on one subject within Astronomy.

Subfields
Planetary Astronomy The study of planets, moons, dwarf planets, comets, and asteroids
Solar Astronomy The study of the Sun
Stellar Astronomy The study of the stars
Galactic Astronomy The study of the Milky Way Galaxy
Extragalactic Astronomy The study of other galaxies
Cosmology The study of the structure of the Universe

Important Individuals Who Advanced Astronomy:

Nicolaus Copernicus (1473-1543) First to propose that the Earth revolves around the Sun

Tycho Brahe (1546-1601) Measurements of the paths of the planets

Johannes Kepler (1571-1630) Planets did not revolve around the Sun in circles but in ellipses

Galieo Galilei (1564-1642) Improvement of the optical telescope and the discovery of Jupiter’s moons

Isaac Newton (1642-1727) Gravity and Newton’s Laws

Albert Einstein (1879-1955) Suggested that the laws of physics apply throughout the universe

Edwin Hubble (1889-1953) Hubble’s Law, the universe is expanding

Carl Sagan (1934-1996) Popularized astronomy

Stephen Hawkings (1942- ) Proposed that as the universe has a beginning that it also has an end
