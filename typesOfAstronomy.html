Types of Astronomy

Galactic Astronomy

Galactic Astronomy is the study of the Milky Way galaxy and all the things inside of it. While Galactic Astronomy is commonly confused with extragalactic astronomy, which deals with everything outside of our galaxy, galactic astronomy only handles the study within it. The Milky Way galaxy is one of the best studied galaxy due to our Solar System belonging in it. While much of the galaxy has been studied and researched, there are still some parts that are still obscured from view due to cosmic dust.

Extragalactic Astronomy

Extragalactic astronomy is the opposite of galactic astronomy. Instead of studying the Milky Way Galaxy and its content, extragalactic astronomy is the study of other galaxies in the universe as well as how galaxies are formed and evolved. This includes the study of the Andromeda Galaxy, one of the nearest galaxy to our own.

Cosmology

Cosmology is the study of the universe as a whole. This includes how the universe was created, like the big bang theory and topics such as how the universe has changed overtime. Cosmology also studies how the universe as a whole will be in the future and how it will affect our galaxies and our planets.
