Electricity

Electricity is similar to charges, and both protons and electrons have a charge. The protons have a positive charge while electrons have a negative charge. Every object surround us have a millions and billions of atoms, and each atom carries a lot of electrons and protons. The protons placed in the middle of the atom, and that area is called the nucleus. The electrons are moving around the outside of the nucleus. Therefore, the electrons that are moving around the nucleus are mainly reliable for electricity.

Electricity is a form of energy that caused by the existence of charged particles such as protons or electrons. The electricity creates many effects, such as lightning, static electricity, electrical current and electromagnetic induction.

Lightning is an electrical discharge from clouds that have collected an electrical charge.


Static electricity is a stationary electric charge that causes sparks or crackling or the attraction of hair or dust.

Electric current is a flow of electric charge. Electromagnetic induction is the production of an electromotive force across a conductor when it is exposed to a varying magnetic field.
