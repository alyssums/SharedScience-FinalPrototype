Hubble Telescope

The Hubble Space Telescope is a space telescope that was launched into Earth's orbit on April 24, 1990 on the STS-31 mission and has remained in operation today. The telescope consists of a 2.4 meter mirror that allows it observe space in near ultraviolet, visible, and near infrared spectra. Because the Hubble Space Telescope's orbit is outside of the Earth's atmosphere, the telescope is able to take high-resolution images with no background light. While the Hubble telescope is not the first space telescope, it is one of the largest and has the most functions and has been a vital research tool and the a public relations boon for astronomy.
